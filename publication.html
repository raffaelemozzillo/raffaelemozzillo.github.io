Jet Propulsion Laboratory

Small Satellite Mission Autonomy

The research period at JPL fell during my second year of PhD (out of three). The objectives for this year were to explore and realize prototypes to evaluate the most promising artificial intelligence algorithms to be applied during all phases of a space mission.
The idea is to develop a methodology (during the third year) for the design of small satellites with enhanced autonomy features.
In particular, I've focused on three technologies:

  • Genetic Algorithms for tradespace exploration
  • Neural Networks for event recognition
  • Fuzzy Logics for failure detection

MIT

CubeSat Mission Concept at Mars

The two months I've spent at MIT we focused on developing innovative mission concepts for distributed systems at Mars, possibly exploiting the CubeSat standard. I have been working in the Sara Seagers's group, in particular with Inamdar, Knapp and Corbin. The collaboration was part of an exchange program in which two students and one professor went to MIT, and three students and one professor came to PoliTo.
My work was inserted in the framework of tradespace exploration, and I helped developing a EDL simulation in order to estimate the attributes of the system design.

ESA

Fly Your Satellite! program

Our second CubeSat, e-st@r II, was selected in the ESA Educational Program Fly Your Satellite!.
As part of the program, I've been involved in the realization of early functional and mission tests for our CubeSat. In addition, me and my colleague participated to the AOCS Crash Course that was held in ESA - ESTEC, that helped us to develop and test the innovative algorithm for the attitude determination that is implemented onboard the satellite.
